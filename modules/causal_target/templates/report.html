Methodology

CausalTarget applies Pearl's do-calculus to distinguish genuine causal drug targets from correlational bystanders. Unlike conventional pipelines that rank by association scores alone, CausalTarget asks: "If we intervene on this protein, does it causally affect the disease outcome?"

  1. Knowledge Graph Assembly — Disease causal graph built from 7 biomedical databases (DisGeNET, Open Targets, KEGG, Reactome, STRING, UniProt, PDB).
  2. Causal Identification — Backdoor criterion applied to test identifiability of causal effects.
  3. Effect Estimation — Average treatment effect estimated via DoWhy (linear regression + IPW).
  4. Sensitivity Analysis — Robustness validated through random common cause, placebo treatment, and data subset refutation tests.
  5. Molecule Generation — Novel candidates generated by GenMol (Variational Autoencoder trained on ChEMBL).
  6. Drug Screening — Candidates screened for drug-likeness (MolScreen) and binding affinity (DockBot).
  7. Composite Scoring — Six-dimensional ranking with causal confidence weighted highest (0.30).
